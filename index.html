The Berlin Statement

Year: 1936 Berlin

In 1936, Adolf Hitler intended to use the Berlin Games as a propaganda tool. Jesse Owens, an African-American grandson of slaves, had other plans.

Owens won not one, but four gold medals (100m, 200m, Long Jump, 4x100m relay). His victories single-handedly dismantled the Nazi ideology on the global stage, proving that excellence is not defined by race but by character and skill.

Philippines' First Gold

Year: 2020 Tokyo

The Perfect 10

Year: 1976 Montreal

Lightning Bolt

Year: 2008 Beijing

The Men's 100m final is the most watched event in the Olympics. In 2008, Usain Bolt didn't just win; he put on a show.

Bolt exploded off the blocks and by the 80m mark, he was so far ahead that he dropped his arms and celebrated before crossing the line. Despite slowing down, he still broke the world record (9.69s).
